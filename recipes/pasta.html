Classic Pasta Bolognese

This classic pasta Bolognese is a rich and savory dish that brings together tender ground meat, aromatic vegetables, and a perfectly seasoned tomato-based sauce. It's a satisfying, comforting meal that is ideal for weeknight dinners or special occasions alike.

The sauce is simmered slowly to deepen the flavor, then served over freshly cooked pasta and topped with a generous sprinkle of Parmesan cheese. Every bite delivers warmth and authentic Italian flavor.

Ingredients

  • 340 g spaghetti or tagliatelle
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 carrot, finely diced
  • 1 celery stalk, finely diced
  • 1 pound (450 g) ground beef or pork (or a mix)
  • 1/4 cup tomato paste
  • 1 (400 g) can crushed tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup beef broth or red wine (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme or basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Grated Parmesan cheese for serving
  • Fresh parsley or basil (optional garnish)

Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add onion, garlic, carrot, and celery. Cook until softened, about 5 to 7 minutes.
  2. Add the ground meat and cook until browned, breaking it up with a spoon as it cooks.
  3. Stir in tomato paste and cook for 1 to 2 minutes. Add crushed tomatoes and broth or wine. Mix well.
  4. Add oregano, thyme or basil, salt, and pepper. Lower heat and let simmer for 20 to 30 minutes, stirring occasionally. Add a splash of water if it gets too thick.
  5. Meanwhile, cook pasta according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  6. Toss the cooked pasta with the Bolognese sauce or serve the sauce on top.
  7. Garnish with grated Parmesan and fresh herbs if desired. Serve warm.
